Wikipedia Trend Analysis During US Presidential Elections

Research Project in fulfilment of Course INFX 575: Data Scaling, Applications and Ethics -- Team: Chmod 777

Contact us with Questions/Suggestions: Akashdeep Jaswal (akashj@uw.edu), Aparna Dharmakkan (aparnard@uw.edu),
Elizabeth MacCready (maccrea@uw.edu), Sumedha Dubey (dubeysum@uw.edu)

Abstract: This research project attempts to look at Wikipedia page view counts for search terms from a variety of fields and find parallel trends with Google trends data, if any, for specific time periods, such as the past 12 months (2015 to 2016) and the past 5 years (2011 to 2016). The purpose of the research is mostly exploratory, and to address curiosities regarding trends on one of these platforms being reflective of the trends witnessed on the other - the null hypothesis being that Wikipedia page view trends are independent of Google search trends. The search terms that were explored included politicians running for current presidential elections, and other famous personalities and terms for recent developments in science and technology. Data was collected from Wikimedia’s PageView API as well as scraped from Wikimedia dumps, which required heavy file processing that was achieved by running Dato’s Graphlab module on Amazon Web Services Hadoop clusters. Aggregation of data to consistent granularities between both platforms helped us create visualizations using D3 that allowed us to find similarities between trends, if any.
